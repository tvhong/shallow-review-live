# Existing Landscape Research

Research into whether our two hackathon ideas already exist somewhere. **TL;DR: partial overlap but nothing fully implements either idea. Both have clear differentiation.**

## Idea 1: Theory of Change across AI Safety Fields

### Closest existing things

- **[AISafety.com Field Map](https://www.aisafety.com/map)** — Interactive map of the AI safety ecosystem with 16 categories of orgs/projects, plus a searchable list view. Focuses on *who's doing what*, not theories of change.
- **[AISafety.world](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/mDG49CJyxzeN99ELz/aisafety-world-is-a-map-of-the-ais-ecosystem)** — Another ecosystem map of orgs and projects.
- **[Map of AI Safety v2](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/rF7MQWGbqQjEkeLJA/map-of-ai-safety-v2)** (LessWrong) — Conceptual map of AI safety research areas and how they relate.
- **[SyDFAIS framework](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/v8SQFy2RLaC5hsyd3/introducing-sydfais-a-systemic-design-framework-for-ai-1)** (EA Forum) — A systemic design framework for AI safety field-building that includes theory of change mapping. Paper/framework, not an interactive tool.
- **["Disentangling AI Alignment" taxonomy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06286)** (arXiv, 2025) — Academic taxonomy distinguishing alignment aim (safety, ethicality, legality), scope (outcome vs execution), and constituency (individual vs collective). Paper, not a tool.
- **[Interactive AI Safety Ecosystem Hub](https://aisafety.davidveksler.com/)** — List and map view of the AI safety ecosystem.

### Gap

None of these explicitly surface and compare **theories of change** across subfields in a navigable, searchable format. Existing maps are org/project-focused, not theory-focused.

## Idea 2: Skill Roadmaps

### Closest existing things

- **[80,000 Hours](https://80000hours.org/ai/)** — Written career reviews, an [AI safety syllabus](https://80000hours.org/articles/ai-safety-syllabus/), and a [list of 67 upskilling resources](https://80000hours.org/2025/06/technical-ai-safety-upskilling-resources/). Static articles, not an interactive roadmap. Also offers 1-on-1 advising (not scalable).
- **[BlueDot Impact](https://bluedot.org/)** — Free AI safety courses with career support. Used by 4,000+ professionals.
- **[ARENA](https://www.arena.education)** — In-person bootcamps for technical AI safety skills (4-5 week programs). Curriculum available online.
- **[AISafety.com Self-Study](https://aisafety.com/courses)** — Curated learning tracks split into two study paths.
- **[AI Safety for Fleshy Humans](https://aisafety.dance/)** — Interactive guide breaking down the AI safety problem into sub-problems.

### Gap

No interactive tool takes your current skills/interests and generates a **personalized roadmap** (mentorship, programs, self-study path) for entering a specific AI safety subfield. 80,000 Hours 1-on-1 advising is closest but not self-serve.

## What this means for us

The combination of both ideas (identify where you fit via theory of change -> get a roadmap to get there) is genuinely novel. Key differentiators:

1. **Theory of change as the organizing principle** — not orgs, not research areas, but *why different approaches think they'll work*
2. **Personalized, self-serve roadmaps** — scaling what 80,000 Hours advisors do manually
3. **The pipeline between the two** — connecting "where should I contribute?" to "how do I get there?"
